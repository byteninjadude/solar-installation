Why Cincinnati Homeowners Choose Solar Energy Solutions

Cincinnati's climate presents unique opportunities and challenges for solar energy production that require local expertise to navigate successfully. The Queen City experiences humid summers with excellent solar potential, averaging 4.4 peak sun hours daily and 57% sunny days throughout the year. However, Cincinnati's snowy winters and variable cloud cover demand proper system design and positioning to maximize year-round energy production.

Duke Energy Ohio Net Metering: Cincinnati residents benefit from Duke Energy Ohio's net metering program, which allows solar customers to receive credits for excess electricity sent back to the grid. While credits are issued at the generation charge rate rather than full retail rates, this still provides meaningful bill reduction opportunities for homeowners.

Ohio offers several state solar incentives, including Solar Renewable Energy Certificates (SRECs) worth $3-5 per megawatt-hour generated, and property tax exemptions for solar installations. Additionally, Cincinnati provides a Property Tax Abatement for Green Buildings program, offering up to 15 years of tax deferrals for energy-efficient home improvements.

The city's permitting process requires building permits and inspections through Cincinnati's Building Department, with specific requirements for solar photovoltaic installations. Our experienced team handles all permit applications and works closely with local inspectors to ensure swift approval and compliance.
